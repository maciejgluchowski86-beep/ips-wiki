# Interacting particle system

An interacting particle system is a continuous-time Markov process on a product configuration space over a countable [lattice](lattice-and-graph.md) \(\Lambda\). Unlike a [spin system](spin-system.md), the single-site state space \(\mathcal S\) need not be two-state and the allowed updates need not be single-site flips.

**References.** Liggett, *Interacting Particle Systems*; Liggett, *Stochastic Interacting Systems*.

## Configuration space

Let \(\Lambda\) be a countable lattice and let \(\mathcal S\) be a finite or countable single-site state space. The configuration space is

$$
\Omega=\mathcal S^\Lambda.
$$

A configuration \(\eta\in\Omega\) assigns a state \(\eta(i)\in\mathcal S\) to each site \(i\in\Lambda\).

## Single-site update form

A common finite-state form has rates \(c_i^a(\eta)\ge0\) for replacing the state at site \(i\) by \(a\in\mathcal S\). Let \(\eta^{i,a}\) be defined by

$$
\eta^{i,a}(j)=
\begin{cases}
a, & j=i,\\
\eta(j), & j\ne i.
\end{cases}
$$

Then the generator acts on [local functions](local-functions.md) by

$$
\cL f(\eta)
=
\sum_{i\in\Lambda}\sum_{a\in\mathcal S} c_i^a(\eta)\bigl(f(\eta^{i,a})-f(\eta)\bigr),
$$

with the convention that terms with \(a=\eta(i)\) may be omitted or assigned rate zero.

## General local update form

More generally, an IPS may use local maps \(\theta:\Omega\to\Omega\), each changing only finitely many coordinates. A formal generator then has the form

$$
\cL f(\eta)
=
\sum_{\theta} r_\theta(\eta)\bigl(f(\theta\eta)-f(\eta)\bigr),
$$

where each rate \(r_\theta\) is local and nonnegative, and the family of maps is locally finite enough for \(\cL f\) to be well-defined on local functions.

## Semigroup

When the generator determines a Markov process \((\eta_t)_{t\ge0}\), its Markov semigroup is denoted by \((P_t)_{t\ge0}\). For bounded measurable \(f\),

$$
P_t f(\eta)=\E_\eta[f(\eta_t)].
$$

Equivalently, if \(P_t(\eta,d\xi)\) is the transition kernel, then

$$
P_t f(\eta)=\int_\Omega f(\xi)P_t(\eta,d\xi).
$$

When the generator is bounded on the relevant finite-dimensional space, this is written formally as \(P_t=e^{t\cL}\). For infinite-volume systems this identity is interpreted through the semigroup generated by the closure of \(\cL\) on an appropriate domain.

## Ergodicity

The long-time behaviour of an IPS is often formulated in terms of invariant measures and [ergodicity](ergodicity.md). In infinite volume, convergence to equilibrium is typically checked against local functions.

## Relation to spin systems

A spin system is the special case \(\mathcal S=\{0,1\}\) with only the flip update \(\eta\mapsto\eta^i\) at each site. Thus every spin system is an interacting particle system, but not every interacting particle system is a spin system.
