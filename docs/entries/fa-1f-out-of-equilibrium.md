# FA-1f out of equilibrium

Out-of-equilibrium results for the [FA-1f model](fa-1f-model.md) describe convergence to the Bernoulli product measure \(\mu_q\) from non-stationary initial laws or deterministic configurations.

**References.** Blondel, Cancrini, Martinelli, Roberto, and Toninelli, *Fredrickson-Andersen one spin facilitated model out of equilibrium*; Mountford and Valle, *Exponential convergence for the Fredrickson-Andersen one spin facilitated model*; Blondel, Deshayes, and Toninelli, *Front evolution of the Fredrickson-Andersen one spin facilitated model*.

## Polynomial-growth graphs

For FA-1f on infinite connected graphs with polynomial growth, Blondel, Cancrini, Martinelli, Roberto, and Toninelli study initial laws \(\nu\ne\mu_q\) under a uniform bound on the mean distance between nearest vacancies. For vacancy density \(q\) above a model-dependent threshold \(\bar q<1\), they prove convergence to equilibrium. The convergence is exponential on \(\Z\) and stretched exponential in higher-dimensional polynomial-growth settings.

## Larger graph classes

Mountford and Valle prove exponential convergence to equilibrium for FA-1f on bounded-degree graphs satisfying a subexponential growth condition. This extends the graph classes for which out-of-equilibrium convergence is known beyond polynomial-growth settings.

## Front evolution on \(\Z\)

For deterministic initial data that are occupied on a half-line, one studies the front, namely the position of the leftmost vacancy. Blondel, Deshayes, and Toninelli prove a law of large numbers and a central limit theorem for the front for \(q\) above a threshold \(\bar q<1\), together with convergence of the process seen from the front to an invariant law.

## Distinctions

These results concern different notions of convergence: convergence from random initial laws, convergence on graph classes, and scaling limits for fronts. They should not be merged into the definition of FA-1f itself.
