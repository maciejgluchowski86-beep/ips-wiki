# Exponential relaxation under confined late interactions

The modified spin system from [undoing duality under confined late interactions](undoing-duality-under-confined-late-interactions.md) is the product of a zero-boundary process on \(R\) and independent two-state chains on \(R^c\). Uniform exponential ergodicity of these components therefore gives exponential convergence of the confined patch term.

## Assumptions

For \(R\Subset\Lambda\), let \(P_t^{R,0}\) be the semigroup of the zero-boundary restriction of the original spin system to \(R\). Assume that there is \(\gamma_1>0\), independent of \(R\), such that for every \(R\Subset\Lambda\), the restricted system has a unique invariant measure \(\nu_R\) and there is \(C_R^0<\infty\) for which

$$
\sup_{\eta\in\{0,1\}^R}
\left|
P_t^{R,0}f(\eta)-\nu_R(f)
\right|
\le
C_R^0e^{-\gamma_1t}\|f\|_\infty
$$

for every bounded \(f\colon\{0,1\}^R\to\mathbb R\).

Set

$$
\gamma_2
=
\inf_{i\in\Lambda}
\inf_{x\in\{0,1\}}
c_i^x(\mathbf0),
\qquad
\gamma
=
\min(\gamma_1,\gamma_2),
$$

and assume \(\gamma_2>0\). The modified generator \(\cL_R\) has invariant measure

$$
\mu_R
=
\nu_R
\otimes
\bigotimes_{i\notin R}
\operatorname{Ber}
\left(
\frac{c_i^0(\mathbf0)}
{c_i^0(\mathbf0)+c_i^1(\mathbf0)}
\right).
$$

## Lemma

For every \(A\Subset\Lambda\), \(T<\infty\), and \(R\Subset\Lambda\), there is \(C_R<\infty\) such that, for every \(t\ge T\),

$$
\sup_{\xi\in\{0,1\}^\Lambda}
\left|
\mathbb E_A
\left[
W_t^\xi\ind(L_{T,t}^R)
\right]
-
\mu_R\left(P_T(\chi_A)\right)
\right|
\le
C_Re^{-\gamma(t-T)}.
$$

In particular, the limit is the same for every initial configuration \(\xi\), and the constant does not depend on \(T\).

## Proof

The process generated by \(\cL_R\) evolves independently on \(R\) and at each site of \(R^c\). The process on \(R\) is uniformly exponentially ergodic with rate \(\gamma_1\), while the chain at \(i\notin R\) is uniformly exponentially ergodic with rate

$$
c_i^0(\mathbf0)+c_i^1(\mathbf0)
\ge
\gamma_2.
$$

Hence \(P_t^R\) is uniformly exponentially ergodic with invariant measure \(\mu_R\) and rate at least \(\gamma\): for some \(C_R<\infty\),

$$
\sup_\xi
\left|
P_t^Rf(\xi)-\mu_R(f)
\right|
\le
C_Re^{-\gamma t}\|f\|_\infty.
$$

By the confined-interaction representation and the bound \(0\le P_T(\chi_A)\le1\),

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\left|
\mathbb E_A
\left[
W_t^\xi\ind(L_{T,t}^R)
\right]
-
\mu_R\left(P_T(\chi_A)\right)
\right|
\\
&\qquad=
\left|
P_{t-T}^RP_T(\chi_A)(\xi)
-
\mu_R\left(P_T(\chi_A)\right)
\right|
\\
&\qquad\le
C_Re^{-\gamma(t-T)}.
\end{aligned}
$$
