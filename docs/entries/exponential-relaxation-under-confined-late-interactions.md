# Exponential relaxation under confined late interactions

The modified spin system from [undoing duality under confined late interactions](undoing-duality-under-confined-late-interactions.md) is the product of a zero-boundary process on \(R\) and independent two-state chains on \(R^c\). Exponential mixing of these components therefore gives exponential convergence of the confined patch term.

## Assumptions

For \(R\Subset\Lambda\), let \(\cL_R^0\) be the zero-boundary restriction of the original spin system to \(R\). Assume that there is \(\gamma_1>0\), independent of \(R\), such that for every \(R\Subset\Lambda\), \(\cL_R^0\) has a unique invariant measure \(\nu_R\) and admits a coupling with its stationary process satisfying

$$
\sup_{\eta\in\{0,1\}^R}
\mathbb P
\left(
\eta_t\ne\widetilde\eta_t
\right)
\le
C_R^0e^{-\gamma_1t}
$$

for some \(C_R^0<\infty\).

Set

$$
\gamma_2
=
\inf_{i\in\Lambda}
\inf_{x\in\{0,1\}}
c_i^x(\mathbf0),
\qquad
\gamma
=
\min(\gamma_1,\gamma_2),
$$

and assume \(\gamma_2>0\). The modified generator \(\cL_R\) has invariant measure

$$
\mu_R
=
\nu_R
\otimes
\bigotimes_{i\notin R}
\operatorname{Ber}
\left(
\frac{c_i^0(\mathbf0)}
{c_i^0(\mathbf0)+c_i^1(\mathbf0)}
\right).
$$

## Lemma

For every \(A\Subset\Lambda\), \(T<\infty\), and \(R\Subset\Lambda\), there is \(C_R<\infty\) such that, for every \(t\ge T\),

$$
\sup_{\xi\in\{0,1\}^\Lambda}
\left|
\mathbb E_A
\left[
W_t^\xi\ind(L_{T,t}^R)
\right]
-
\mu_R\left(P_T(\chi_A)\right)
\right|
\le
C_Re^{-\gamma(t-T)}.
$$

In particular, the limit is the same for every initial configuration \(\xi\), and the constant does not depend on \(T\).

## Proof

By the confined-interaction representation,

$$
\mathbb E_A
\left[
W_t^\xi\ind(L_{T,t}^R)
\right]
=
P_{t-T}^R P_T(\chi_A)(\xi).
$$

The process generated by \(\cL_R\) evolves independently on \(R\) and at each site of \(R^c\). Couple the process on \(R\) to equilibrium using the assumed coupling, and couple each exterior two-state chain to its stationary version using the same update clocks. The coupling rate is at least

$$
\min(\gamma_1,\gamma_2)=\gamma,
$$

so for some \(C_R<\infty\),

$$
\sup_\xi
\mathbb P
\left(
\xi_t^R\ne\widetilde\xi_t^R
\right)
\le
C_Re^{-\gamma t},
$$

where \(\widetilde\xi_t^R\) is stationary with law \(\mu_R\). Since

$$
0\le P_T(\chi_A)\le1,
$$

it follows that

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\left|
P_{t-T}^R P_T(\chi_A)(\xi)
-
\mu_R\left(P_T(\chi_A)\right)
\right|
\\
&\qquad\le
\mathbb P
\left(
\xi_{t-T}^R\ne\widetilde\xi_{t-T}^R
\right)
\\
&\qquad\le
C_Re^{-\gamma(t-T)}.
\end{aligned}
$$

This proves the claim.
