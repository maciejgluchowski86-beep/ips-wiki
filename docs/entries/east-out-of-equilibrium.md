# East out of equilibrium

Out-of-equilibrium results for the [East model](east-model.md) use the model's oriented structure. The main question is convergence to \(\mu_q\) from initial laws or configurations that are not already at equilibrium.

**References.** Cancrini, Martinelli, Schonmann, and Toninelli, *Facilitated oriented spin models: some non equilibrium results*; Marêché, *Exponential convergence to equilibrium for the \(d\)-dimensional East model*; Hartarsky and Toninelli, *Kinetically constrained models*.

## One-dimensional East model

For the one-dimensional East model, Cancrini, Martinelli, Schonmann, and Toninelli prove exponential convergence to equilibrium for broad classes of non-equilibrium initial laws. In particular, convergence holds for initial laws concentrated on configurations with no forever-blocked site and for Bernoulli product initial laws away from the fully occupied degenerate case.

In the current convention, \(0\) is the facilitating state. The fully occupied configuration has no vacancies and is therefore blocked for East-type constraints.

## Higher-dimensional East model

Marêché proves exponential convergence to equilibrium for the \(d\)-dimensional East model in the settings where convergence is possible. The proof uses the oriented structure of the model and extends earlier stretched-exponential convergence results.

## Oriented mechanism

The key feature is that the East model is an [oriented spin system](oriented-spin-system.md): update constraints point in one direction and information does not flow back. This structure makes it possible to control relaxation by building equilibrium regions from the facilitating direction.

## Separation from equilibrium time scales

This page records out-of-equilibrium convergence. Equilibrium relaxation-time asymptotics, spectral gaps, and finite-volume mixing estimates belong on [KCSM relaxation and mixing](kcsm-relaxation-and-mixing.md) or model-specific time-scale pages.
